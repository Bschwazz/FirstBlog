ACM TechNews Article Summary:

Encryption breaking quantum computers getting closer:

Quantum computers are making a speedy entrance into the world of computer science, with public and private research happening all over the world for their advancement. Although there are many good things that can come from quantum computing, there are also quite a few bad things as well. Quantum computers use bits that can be either a 0 OR a 1, these are known as qubits; regular computers use regular bits that can be only 1 or 0. The negative side of a quantum computer would be the risks that it has on cyber security. Studies from last years "Quantum Safe Workshop" in Toronto predicted that by 2026 there was a 1/7 chance of a quantum computer cracking RSA-2048 encryption, this year that prediction is now a 1/6 chance. Overall, quantum computers will bring a whole new realm of computing to the world of bits, and it will be interesting to see how far it goes.
